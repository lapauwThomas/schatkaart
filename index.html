We're a not-for-profit network of citizens offering free camping spots in their gardens to slow travellers!

Welcome to My Garden connects citizens who want to offer their garden to slow travellers who need a basic camping spot for the night. This initiative is not-for-profit and has been set up by a small group of volunteers - mostly outdoor enthusiasts and some indoor enthusiasts (i.e. developers).

Step 1: Sign up to become or find a host

If you want to open up your garden, please click the Add your garden button. Pick your garden of choice and contact your host! Arrange your date and time of arrival and off you go!

Step 2: Plan your journey

After you’ve made arrangements with your host, walk or bike to your camping spot. Introduce yourself, pitch your tent for the night, and continue on your hiking or biking trail the next day. And have a great time, of course!

Step 3: Pitch your tent and have fun!

Last breakfast together before a final ride of 60 km back to Brussels. Farewell drink to say goodbye and see you next year!

All you need to know

Please read this FAQ section thoroughly so that you know what you're getting yourself into. If you can't find the answer on your question below, please drop us an email at welcometomygardennetwork[at]gmail.com.

  • Why Welcome to my Garden

    Exploring by bicycle makes you feel alive. It's a unique way to discover a region. What if you combine this with meeting other enthusiasts and have a great time together?

    The Welcome to My Garden gives you the opportunity to travel by bicycle in Belgium and meet new people.

  • For whom?

    For both curious-minded people who want to discover bicycle touring and experienced bicycle travellers to share their passion and stories.

    From solo travelers to families, everyone is welcome. If you are travelling with your 10 best friends, we doubt you’ll still have time to meet others, so no groups please!

  • Equipment

    Come with your own bicycle. Please note that you'll carry your own panniers with camping gear (tent, sleeping bag & mattress) and repair kit.

    Yet, not owning equipment shouldn't stop you from participating, ask a friend to lend you equipment or leave a comment in the registration form so that we can help you.

  • Participation fee

    50 euro per person (25 euro per kid & free of charge for children under 2 years old). The fee includes 2x breakfast, 2x dinner, insurance, social activities, basic facilities & some other small costs (overhead). Lunch & drinks are not included.

    Check out the full budget here.

  • Itinerary

    3 days of cycling 50 to 60 km. We won't cycle together in 1 big group. It's a 3-day event and it's not possible to join for only 1 day. You'll receive more information about the itinerary before the event and on the first day during the briefing.

  • Activities

    In the evening there will be social activities around bicycle touring. Please let us know about your suggestions in the registration form! Those activities won't be next to the tents, so that you can also rest if you want to.

  • Organisation

    The festival is a not-for-profit project organised by volunteers. During the festival all partipants are invited to volunteer and give a hand in either the morning or evening.

  • Insurance

    There's a liability and physical damage insurance during the event. Yet, you participate at your own risk and responsibility. You will be asked to sign a disclaimer on the first day to wave any liability towards the organising volunteers and must respect the highway code.

  • Code of conduct

    The goal of the Welcome to My Garden is to meet new people and get to know each other. It's a must to be open towards others and to agree with our code of conduct.

  • Languages

    The Welcome to My Garden is organised à la belge: a good mix of English, French, Dutch and German. Whatever language you speak, we'll find a way to communicate with one another.
